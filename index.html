Hey, I'm Ning. Nice to meet you!

I'm a fourth-year Ph.D. student at the School of Computing and Information, University of Pittsburgh. I work with Dr. Daqing He in Information Retrieval, Integration and Synthesis (iRiS) Lab, where I study Human Information Behavior, Human Data Interaction and Health Informatics.I also work with Dr. Bo Xie from University of Texas at Austin.

My research aims to comprehend people and data interaction in the context of the evolving networked environments. Specifically, my work explores how emerging intelligent and social information technologies (e.g., Internet of Things, smart devices) impact the interactions between individuals and their personal health related data/information, from which I identify the underlying challenges and needs related to the interactions.

The overaching goal of my research is to advance people's capabilities of accessing and understanding to their personal health data/information with the support of such cutting-edge technologies. To achieve this goal, I use both qualitative (e.g., interviews), quantitative (e.g., surveys), and human-centered design (e.g., participatory design, co-design) methods approaches to understand the issues of people's intentions and behaivors to the personal health-related data/information. I apply these approaches to various health contexts, specifically in populations with chronic conditions.
